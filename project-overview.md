# Projet de vie / Etudes: Brief Overview

## Core Idea

Etudes is an iOS-oriented digital humanities project about memory, self-writing, mental cartography, and practices of self-formation. It should feel less like a productivity app and more like a space for drift, attention, recollection, and recurring intellectual exercises.

## Main Modules

- Map: a mental and autobiographical cartography of places, events, memories, people, images, melodies, texts, and encounters with theories or authors.
- Practices: a curated collection of cultural and philosophical self-practices, including the Proust questionnaire, stream of consciousness, exercises from Foucault, Sloterdijkian self-intensification, literary routines, and associative writing.
- Letters: a slow epistolary/social mode, with constrained frequency and selected visibility, positioned against instant messaging.
- Traces: a layer for return, recurrence, links, and revisiting previous notes or states.
- Drift: a mode where random fragments, prompts, memories, places, or practices surface without an overly rational structure.

## Theoretical Frame

The strongest framing is: not "an app for journaling", but an experimental DH project that studies how aesthetic interfaces can turn memory, writing, and self-practices into a digital humanistic archive.

Recurring references and concepts:

- Foucault: techniques/practices of the self.
- Sloterdijk: anthropotechnics, self-intensification, vertical tension, exercise.
- Proust: questionnaire, memory, self-description.
- Rhizome/cartography: equality of fragments, nonlinear relations, places as time.
- Epistolary culture: slow communication, personal letters, mediated self-writing.
- Shanshui/hiddenness: partial revelation, atmospheric backgrounds, concealed forms.

## Product Direction

The app should combine meditation, writing, nonlinear thought, and archive. Its distinction from competitors is the union of drift and structure: Flowstate/ZenJournal give flow, Obsidian/Notion give traces and structure, Muse/Are.na give visual nonlinearity, but none combine all of these with cultural practices and memory cartography.

Important UX principles:

- meditativeness rather than productivity;
- tactile pleasure, sound, subtle visual atmosphere;
- possibility to "fall into" the interface;
- rules learned through practice, not heavy explanation;
- effort as engagement, not friction;
- return to traces without destroying drift;
- randomness as a way of surfacing memory and association.

## DH Requirements To Strengthen

- Define a research question.
- Treat the practice collection as a curated corpus with sources and metadata.
- Give map entries metadata and categories.
- Add export, visualization, or analysis possibilities.
- Make privacy and ethics explicit, since the project handles intimate memory.
- Show what knowledge the project produces beyond individual experience.

## Near-Term Practical Focus

- Build the simplest working iOS prototype in Swift.
- Prioritize three core surfaces: map, practices, letters.
- Keep the architecture modular so incomplete practice blocks can be added safely.
- Prototype concrete flows before expanding theory further.
- Prepare a version that can be shown as a DH project, thesis artifact, and product experiment.
